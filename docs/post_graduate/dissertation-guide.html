Core Chapters

Chapter 1: Introduction Structure

  • Background of Study: General context & existing knowledge
  • Need/Rationale: Why research is important
  • Statement of Problem: Specific problem you‚Äôre investigating
  • Scope & Delimitation: Boundaries of your research
  • Expected Outcomes: Anticipated benefits

Chapter 2: Writing SMART Objectives

Primary Objective Single, well-defined main goal

Secondary Objectives Additional exploratory/sub-aims

SMART Criteria - Specific - Measurable - Achievable - Relevant - Time-bound

Chapter 3: Literature Review Strategy

  • Search Strategy: PubMed, Google Scholar, JSTOR databases
  • Search Terms: Keywords, MeSH terms, inclusion/exclusion
  • Organization: Thematic approach (not just summaries)
  • Synthesis: Compare studies, identify gaps, build narrative
  • Critical Analysis: Evaluate strengths, limitations, quality

Chapter 4a: Study Design Selection

Observational Designs - Cross-sectional - Case-control - Cohort - Descriptive

Qualitative Designs - Phenomenology - Grounded theory - Ethnography

Experimental Designs - RCT - Quasi-experimental - Before-after

Justification Explain why this design is best for your research question

Chapter 4b: Sample Selection & Size Calculation

Aspect Details
Inclusion Criteria Clear criteria for who participates
Exclusion Criteria Conditions that disqualify participation
Sample Size Formula Power 80%, Œ±=0.05 with parameter justification
Sampling Method Random, stratified, systematic, or purposive

Chapter 4c: Ethical Considerations & Data Collection

Mandatory Requirements: - ‚úì Ethical Approval from Institutional Ethics Committee - ‚úì Informed Consent Form (Hindi/English) - ‚úì Confidentiality & anonymization plan - ‚úì Pilot Study (10% sample) - ‚úì Validity & Reliability measures - ‚úì Data Collection Procedure (step-by-step)

Chapter 4d: Statistical Analysis Plan

  • Descriptive Statistics: Mean, SD, frequencies, percentages
  • Inferential Tests: t-test, chi-square, ANOVA
  • Significance Level: p < 0.05 (a priori specification)
  • Software Used: R, SPSS, Stata (with version)
  • Missing Data: Handling strategy documented

Chapter 5: Results - Data Presentation

Tables - Numbered sequentially - Clear headings - Summary below

Text Descriptions - Present key findings - Statistical significance

Figures - Professional quality - Colored when appropriate - Descriptive captions

No Interpretation Discussion goes in Discussion chapter

Chapter 6: Discussion - Analysis & Interpretation

  • Key Findings Summary: Recap main results in context of objectives
  • Literature Comparison: How findings align/differ from existing research
  • Explanation of Results: Why results occurred (mechanisms, theories)
  • Limitations: Be honest about study limitations
  • Implications: Practical, policy, and research implications

Chapter 7: Conclusions & Recommendations

Key Conclusions Brief synthesis of main findings

Recommendations (Practical & Policy) Actionable steps for programs & policy

Future Research Directions for further investigation

References: Vancouver Style

Vancouver Style: Overview & Key Features

  • Numbered Citations: Use [1], [2], [3] in text in order of appearance
  • Reference List: At end of manuscript, numbered to match in-text
  • Standard Format: Author(s). Title. Source. Year;Volume(Issue):Pages
  • Wide Acceptance: Standard in biomedical, public health, medical research

Data Management Best Practices

  • Data Organization: Maintain master data sheet with participant IDs & variables
  • Backup Systems: Keep multiple copies on external drives or cloud storage
  • Confidentiality: Use codes instead of names in datasets
  • Version Control: Save drafts with dates (Dissertation_v1, v2, v3)
  • Data Cleaning: Document corrections, validate entries before analysis
  • Analysis Trail: Document all statistical procedures performed

Academic Integrity & Plagiarism Prevention

Effective Communication with Your Guide

  • Regular Meetings: Schedule fortnightly or monthly meetings
  • Preparation: Come with specific questions and completed sections
  • Feedback Implementation: Incorporate suggestions promptly
  • Timeline Discussion: Establish clear deadlines for each chapter
  • Acknowledge Help: Thank guide in acknowledgments section
  • Professional Conduct: Maintain respectful, collegial communication

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • ‚úó Too Broad Research Question: Narrow scope for achievable objectives
  • ‚úó Inadequate Literature Review: Must demonstrate knowledge gap thoroughly
  • ‚úó Methodological Flaws: Improper sampling, uncontrolled confounders
  • ‚úó Poor Data Quality: Inconsistent collection, missing values
  • ‚úó Weak Discussion: Simply repeating results without interpretation
  • ‚úó Over-generalization: Claims beyond study scope or sample
  • ‚úó Procrastination: Starting late = rushed writing = low quality
